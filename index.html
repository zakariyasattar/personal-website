Test-Lock

  Test-Lock began as an idea that I had when I noticed that most of the available testing applications were desktop applications and very tricky to use and navigate. I prided myself in the fact that Test-Lock was the only in-browser testing solution on the market at the time. I began working on Test-Lock in the summer after I got out of school, and after about 3 months, I had close to a finished product, just in time for the start of the new school year. This was a project that absolutely revolutionized my skill set. After this project, I came out with deep knowledge of working with JS and HTML to make dynamic webpages and with a brand new skills in database creation and management I was also forced to learn how to store and organize data locally using JSON and SessionStorage. Overall, this is the biggest project I've ever worked on, and while it never got used by any schools officially, I am beyond grateful for this opportunity because of how much I learned.

 Both the code and a video demo walkthrough are available on GitHub: Test-Lock

Project 2
Niles West Bus App

  The Niles West Bus App first came into fruition as an idea proposal submitted to the App Development Club The problem was that the school busses would arrive in random spots every day, and there was only one person that new the locations of all the bus: the security guard. So, without fail, every day hundreds of students would swamp this security guard asking where their bus number was. Our app was an easy solution to a big problem. As the president of the App Development Club, I was tasked with the creation of the app. Within a week of the idea being presented to the club, another student and I had finished the app. It was beautiful, to this day I've never worked on a project that has been so simple and had such an impact on the daily lives of so many people. When this project was officially launched, I really began to understand the level of impact that was possible through my work as a Computer Scientist.
